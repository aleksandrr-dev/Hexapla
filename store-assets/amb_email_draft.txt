Subject: Ang Malayang Biblia in a free offline Bible app — which release is stable, and a question about renderings

Dear Ang Malayang Biblia team,

My name is Aleksandr Ratchkov. I develop Hexapla, a free offline
parallel Bible app for Android — no ads, no accounts, no data
collection, nothing locked (https://aleksandrr-dev.github.io/Hexapla/).
It carries 22 classic translations in the Textus Receptus tradition
(KJV 1611, Luther 1545, Reina-Valera 1909, the Greek Byzantine NT, the
Hebrew Tanakh, and others), with a tap-any-word interlinear for the
original languages.

I have been looking for a Tagalog translation in the TR tradition for
a long time, and yours is the only one that exists: I verified the AMB
against our usual test verses and it passes every one — 1 Timothy 3:16
("ang Diyos ay nagpakita na nakabalot sa laman"), 1 John 5:7, Acts
8:37, Luke 2:33, John 3:13, Romans 16:24, Acts 20:28. The word-level
Strong's tagging is remarkable for a project of any size. Thank you
for making it CC BY-SA — I would of course credit "Ang Malayang
Biblia by Open Bibles" (or any wording you prefer) with the license
and a link in the app's Text sources section.

Before including it I wanted to ask three things:

1. Which release do you consider stable for redistribution? I have
   the "Ang Malayang Biblia (Bagong Testamento)" release of March
   2025 (TheWordAMB.nt). I saw work on a revision in the repository —
   would you rather I wait for it, or ship the current release and
   update later?

2. Hexapla's other translations are historic texts, so readers expect
   traditional renderings. Two AMB choices stand out in that company:
   modern currency inside the verse text (Luke 19:13, "500,000 piso")
   and some de-transliterated vocabulary ("Kongregasyon",
   "lublob"). Is there — or do you plan — an edition with the
   traditional renderings (minas, iglesia, bautismo)? If not, I may
   still include the AMB as it is, clearly presented as a modern TR
   translation; I simply want to represent your work the way you
   intend it.

3. Anything else you would want a redistributor to know?

Everything in Hexapla is free forever — the mission is simply to put
the Scriptures in as many hands as possible.

Maraming salamat po, and God bless your work,
