Dear Icelandic Bible Society,

My name is Aleksandr Ratchkov. I develop Hexapla, a free offline
parallel Bible app for Android with no ads and no data collection
(https://aleksandrr-dev.github.io/Hexapla/). It carries classic
national Bibles — the KJV 1611, Luther 1545, Karl XII:s Bibel 1703,
the Danish 1819/1871 — and I would be honoured to include the
Gudbrandsbiblia 1584, which you publish at
https://biblian.is/gudbrandsbiblia/.

I wrote to you recently about this; since then, Thordis
Ulfarsdottir at Arnastofnun has kindly pointed me to your site as
the authoritative digital text (and warned me away from the flawed
archive.org copy), so I can now ask three much more specific
questions:

Three questions:

1. May I include your transcription in the app? Everything in it is
   free for everyone, forever, and the source would be credited in
   the app's "Text sources" section exactly as you wish.

2. The web pages present the prose in pericope paragraphs without
   verse numbers (faithful to the 1584 printing). Do the working
   files prepared by Jon Hjorleifur Stefansson exist in a
   verse-tagged or otherwise structured form? A structured source
   would let the app align the 1584 text verse-by-verse with the
   other translations. If it is easier, I would be glad to be put in
   touch with him directly.

3. If the 1584 text proves impractical, would the same permission
   extend to the Videyjarbiblia 1841 (biblian.is/videy/)?

Thank you for making these treasures of Icelandic Christianity
available.
