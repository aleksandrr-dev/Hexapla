To: Fr. Aleksandr Andreev (Ponomar Project maintainer — GitHub @typiconman;
    contact via ponomar.net / the address listed in the repo)
Subject: Permission question — Elizabeth Bible psalm superscriptions in a free Bible app

Dear Father Aleksandr,

Christ is in our midst!

I am the developer of Hexapla, a free offline parallel-Bible app for Android
(https://aleksandrr-dev.github.io/Hexapla/). It exists for evangelism: no ads,
no analytics, nothing paid or locked, all texts public domain or permissively
licensed with attribution.

The app ships the 1751 Elizabeth Bible in modernized (civil) spelling, derived
from the CrossWire CSlElizabeth module. That digitization is missing most of
the psalm superscriptions (надписания) — Ps 22 «Псалом Давиду», the «Песнь
степеней» of Ps 119-133, and so on. Your Ponomar Project's Psalter
(Ponomar/languages/cu/bible/elis/Psalm.text) is the only machine-readable
Elizabeth text I have found that preserves them completely, and I would be very
glad to restore them from it.

My questions:

1. The ponomar repository is licensed GPL-3.0. May the BIBLE TEXT files —
   specifically the psalm superscriptions, transliterated into the civil
   orthography our asset already uses (which removes the accentuation layer) —
   be used in a free, non-GPL app, with credit to the Ponomar Project in the
   app's text-sources section? I want to honor your terms exactly, so I am
   asking rather than relying on my own reading of how the GPL applies to
   1751 text data.

2. If it is simpler, would you consider an explicit statement (public domain
   or CC0/CC-BY) covering the Bible-text data files, as distinct from the
   Ponomar software?

The app would credit the Ponomar Project by name with a link, in all interface
languages, alongside our other source attributions.

Thank you for the enormous service Ponomar renders to the Church — and for
your time.

With gratitude in Christ,
