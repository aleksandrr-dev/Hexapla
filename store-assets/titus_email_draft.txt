Dear Professor Gippert,

I develop Hexapla, a free offline parallel-Bible app for Android (no ads,
no analytics, nothing paid; https://aleksandrr-dev.github.io/Hexapla/).
It pairs classical Bible translations across languages — currently 29
translations in 24 languages, all public domain or CC-licensed, each
credited in the app.

TITUS hosts the only machine-readable, verse-structured digitizations I
have found of two texts I would dearly like to offer readers:

  1. the Zohrab Bible (classical Armenian, Venice 1805), and
  2. the 1743 Moscow ("Bakar") Georgian Bible.

Your pages state that no parts may be republished without prior
permission by the copyright holder — so I am writing to ask exactly that
permission: to convert the plain verse text (without your apparatus or
markup) into the app's format and distribute it inside this free app,
with TITUS and the project credited in the app's sources section in all
its interface languages.

If permission depends on conditions (specific credit wording, a notice,
a restriction to the plain text), I will gladly follow them. And if the
answer is no, I fully respect that — thank you in any case for the
extraordinary service TITUS has done for these texts.

With respect and appreciation,
